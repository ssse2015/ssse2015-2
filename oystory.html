New York Harbor contains half of the world's oysters. Oysters have the ability to filter the water that they are in. One oyster could filter up to 50 gallons of water.They fear the blue crab and snails, but their real killer is polluted water and toxic water.




Harbor oyster shells measured up to 10 inches, and early European travellers describe the shellfish as being about a foot in length..

When people learned oysters were edible, seafood restaurants opened and oysters were harvested by people everywhere. Trash was also dumped into the water, killing off many oysters. The Clean Water Act of 1972, made it illegal for people to dump trash into the water without a permit. (Learn more.) Due to the dirty water, oysters could not even be eaten, nor could they filter water. The Billion Oyster Project was formed in 2010 by Murray Fisher to allow oysters to return to their natural habitats and gradually restore the water to its original state.
